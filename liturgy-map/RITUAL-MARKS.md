## The pivot, in one paragraph

No RISE / SIT / SUNG glyphs. The rabbi announces communal movement live — the book doesn't need to. What the margin carries instead are **ritual marks**: small, unobtrusive cues at the inherited embodied practices that *nobody announces* — the bows, the covered eyes, the small steps, the raised cup. Form: a tiny gold glyph in the gutter + a 4–8 word micro-label. Voice: **"some of us…"** — inherited practice freely offered, zero prescription. A mark never tells anyone to do anything; it tells them what some hands and bodies around them are doing, and quietly why.

## How to read this inventory

- **Anchor** = the exact line/word in the current `build/typst/content/*.typ` draft where the gold glyph sits.
- **Micro-label** = the proposed 4–8 word gutter text, in the "some of us" register. All labels are proposals; Daniel owns the final wording.
- **Source** = carried over from `CHOREOGRAPHY-MAP.md` unless marked *(new lookup)*. SA OC = Shulchan Aruch Orach Chayim; MB = Mishnah Berurah; AhS = Arukh HaShulchan.
- **Confidence:** `[high]` codified law verified · `[med]` attested custom or widespread practice · `[low]` varies, needs Daniel's call.
- **☐ Daniel** — approval column, Daniel's alone; no agent may fill a ☐.
- Excluded: anything that is pure posture direction (rise/sit — announced live), pure voice direction (sung/spoken texture — a setlist fact), and any ritual with no Friday-evening anchor (listed at the end with reasons).

**Bowing mechanics, once, for the legend page (not repeated per mark):** bend the knees at *Baruch*, bow the body at *atah*, straighten before the divine Name (MB 113:12; Kitzur SA 18:11 — carried, verified). At Modim, head and body together, "like a reed" (MB 113:12).

## 1 · Kabbalat Shabbat

### M1 — Candle lighting: light first, shield, then bless

| Anchor | Practice | Micro-label | Source | Conf. | ☐ Daniel |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| The blessing "Barúch atá Adonai… l'hadlik ner shel Shabbat" (candle unit) | The lights are kindled *first*, then eyes or hands shield the flame, and only then comes the blessing — so the blessing still precedes its benefit even though the act came first. | *some shield their eyes, then bless* (6) | Rema, SA OC 263:5 — carried, verified | [high] | ☐ |

The inversion is the whole teaching: this is the one blessing in the service said *after* its act, and the shielded eyes are the hinge. A natural candidate for a companion commentary footnote.

### M2 — L'cha Dodi, final verse: greeting the bride

| Anchor | Practice | Micro-label | Source | Conf. | ☐ Daniel |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| "Bó'i v'shalóm…" (lcha-dodi.typ:39), glyph landing at the three-fold "bó'i chalá, bó'i chalá… Shabbat malk'tá" | Facing the entrance, some dip or bow at each *bo'i chalah* — greeting Shabbat the bride the way Rabbi Chanina and Rabbi Yannai walked out to greet the queen. | *some bow to greet Shabbat the bride* (7) | Turn to the door/west: AhS OC 242:40; Shabbat 119a:2 — carried, verified [high]. The bow itself: widespread attested custom, **not** codified in SA/AhS — carried honestly [med] | [med] | ☐ |

**Flag:** the rise and the turn are communal movement — the rabbi almost certainly announces them live, so they are *not* what this mark carries. The mark teaches the WHY (the bride, the Safed field-walkers) and the unannounced bow. If Daniel prefers, the label can lean harder on the why: *"the mystics walked out to meet her"* (7) — though that drifts from the "some…" register.

## 3 · Amidah

### M5 — Three small steps in

| Anchor | Practice | Micro-label | Source | Conf. | ☐ Daniel |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| "Adonai, s'fatái tiftách" / rubric of "We Open Our Lips" (amidah-silent.typ, unit start) | Some take three small steps forward before beginning — approaching, the way you'd step toward someone you need to speak with. | *some take three small steps in* (6) | Rokeach gloss at SA OC 95:1 — carried, verified (the gloss offers it as optional practice); widespread custom | [med] | ☐ |

**No closing-steps anchor — deliberate.** Daniel omitted Oseh Shalom from the silent Amidah text (see CHOREOGRAPHY-MAP §3, "TEXT GAP" row, and Divergence 4). The traditional three-steps-back with left/right/forward bows (SA OC 123:1 — carried, verified) therefore has **nothing to sit on, and no mark is proposed. Do not invent one.** If Daniel ever adds the Oseh Shalom line, a closing mark becomes a one-row addition here.

### M6 — Feet together

| Anchor | Practice | Micro-label | Source | Conf. | ☐ Daniel |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Same anchor as M5 — "Adonai, s'fatái tiftách" | Some stand with feet together for the whole prayer — one single foot, like the angels of Ezekiel's vision. | *some stand feet together, like the angels* (7) | SA OC 95:1 — carried, verified | [high] | ☐ |

**Layout flag:** M5 and M6 share one anchor line. Two glyphs + two labels in the same gutter slot will crowd a ~0.27″ gutter. Options for Daniel: (a) stack the two marks, (b) merge into one label — e.g. *"some step in, feet together, like angels"* (7) — with the split explained on the legend page, or (c) keep M6 and move M5's label to the legend. Recommend (b).

### M7–M10 — The four bows of the Amidah

The only four bows the tradition assigns — one who adds bows at other blessings is taught not to (SA OC 113:1 — carried, verified). No marks anywhere else in the Amidah.

| # | Anchor | Practice | Micro-label | Source | Conf. | ☐ Daniel |

Noting it so Daniel sees the reasoning.

## Back matter

### M13 — Kiddush: the raised cup

| Anchor | Practice | Micro-label | Source | Conf. | ☐ Daniel |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rubric "We raise the cup of wine and bless" / "Barúch atá… boréi p'rí hagáfen" (kiddush.typ) | One person raises the cup and blesses for everyone — all who listen and answer *amen* have made Kiddush. | *one cup, raised, blesses us all* (6) | Remember Shabbat over wine: Pesachim 106a — carried, cited in draft commentary; listeners fulfill through the one who blesses — carried | [high] | ☐ |

**Register flag:** this is the one mark that can't honestly say "some of us" — the cup has a single pair of hands. Proposed label keeps the invitational warmth by making the *congregation* the object. Alternate in-register option, from the Rema at SA OC 271:10 (carried, verified): *some watch the candlelight through the wine* (7) — the custom of looking at the candles during Kiddush. Daniel picks one; two marks on the Kiddush page would be one too many.
