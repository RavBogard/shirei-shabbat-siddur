## Title (settled, 2026-07-03)

**Shirei Shabbat · Songs of Shabbat** — by Rabbi Daniel Bogard (Central Reform Congregation).
(Supersedes the working name "Shir Shabbat." Hebrew: שִׁירֵי שַׁבָּת.)

## Guiding principles (Daniel, standing)

- **Kavanot come mostly from the rabbis, live.** The book should NOT be thick with pre-written
  spiritual intentions. The added/teaching layer is **structural & educational** (what a prayer is,
  where it sits in the frame, brief history) — leaving room for the rabbi's live framing.
- **The document is accessible, educational, usable, and flexible** above all.
- **Reference texts people 'flip to' live at the back** (e.g., Kiddush).
- **Base Hebrew on a standard Conservative siddur** (Lev Shalem / Sim Shalom): full traditional,
  egalitarian (imahot), Sephardi. **Ease off particularism** — do NOT cut particularist lines; the
  book's distinctiveness lives in the English voice, transliteration, and design, not in trimming
  the Hebrew. (Daniel, 2026-07-03)
- **Rubrics/stage directions: light, TRADITIONAL-based** (traditional bowing/rising points), not
  Reform conventions. **Call-and-response: subtle typographic cue** (no "Leader/All" labels).
- **Aleinu: the standard Conservative-siddur text** (keep it as printed there).
- **Priestly Blessing: single degendered threefold blessing + blessing-of-children framing.**

## Margin-System Decision Walkthrough (Daniel, 2026-07-05)

Daniel reviewed the margin-system implementation plan and locked in final design decisions.

### 1. CHOREOGRAPHY GLYPHS — MAJOR PIVOT: DROPPED & REPLACED BY RITUAL MARKS

**Rationale:** Communal movement (rise/sit/bow for Bar'chu, Amidah, Aleinu, etc.) is announced live
by the rabbi every week — printed cues in the book are redundant and prescriptive. The book should
invite, not command.

**New direction — RITUAL MARKS:** Replace choreography glyphs with small gold glyphs + 4–8 word
micro-labels in the gutter, reserved for **inherited embodied practices nobody announces** — the
unspoken, universal moves every regular knows: steps into the Amidah, bows within the Amidah,
eyes covered at Sh'ma, tzitzit held during V'ahavta, L'cha Dodi turn, etc. Voice: "some of us..."
invitations, zero prescription. No glyph legend (glyphs *are* the content, not code to decipher).

**New inventory file:** `liturgy-map/RITUAL-MARKS.md` (DRAFT, awaiting Daniel's approval once filled).
**Deprecation:** `liturgy-map/CHOREOGRAPHY-MAP.md` becomes an archive; Phase 1 R1 retired from the critical path.
**First-occurrence label logic:** Retired — ritual-mark labels are **always shown** (not first-occurrence-only); they are the content of the gutter, not annotations.

Attribution: Daniel, margin-system decision walkthrough.

### 2. SH'MA POSTURE

**Settled:** No cue printed. The room does what it does (sitting, standing, or mixed). The prayer's
power is in the text and intention, not in a prescribed posture.

Attribution: Daniel.

### 3. MOURNER'S KADDISH — RUBRIC WORDING

**Settled:** Rubric reads: "Mourners rise; all who wish may stand with them." (Glyph N/A after the
pivot — communal standing is announced live by the rabbi.)

**Settled:** Oseh Shalom is **deliberately NOT added** to the end of the silent Amidah

is intentional; the two-movement structure (Amidah → Concluding, separated) stands.
