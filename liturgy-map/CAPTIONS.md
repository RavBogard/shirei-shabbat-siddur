Each caption is the one-line italic "what's happening now" line that hangs below the accordion, behind the short gold rule, and changes per unit. Voice per `01_STYLE_GUIDE.md` "Translation voice": intimate, embodied, historically grounded, never academic, never instructional — "we…" not "you should…". Each ≤ 20 words. HaShem conventions observed (no captions name the divine except by the book's own idiom).

**Dedupe rule:** every caption must say something the unit's kavanah does *not* already say. The third column quotes the kavanah's opening words as the check — read caption and kavanah side by side; if they land the same point, the caption fails.

## Report to Daniel

### Units where the kavanah already does the caption's job (candidates for a shorter caption)

These are the tightest dedupe cases — the kavanah opens on the same idea the caption wants, so the caption has to work hard to stay clear of it. Consider trimming the caption to a bare orienting phrase and letting the kavanah carry the meaning:

1. **Modim.** The kavanah already states the two load-bearing facts — the bow and "the one blessing a person must speak in their own voice." My caption reuses both. A shorter caption ("*A slight bow, and thanks — the everyday miracles named one at a time.*") would clear the overlap and pick up the "daily / every day" thread the kavanah touches only at its end.
2. **Shalom Rav.** Kavanah opens "The Amidah ends where it has been reaching all along — shalom." My caption says nearly the same. Shorten to something about the *evening* form specifically (the shorter sibling of Sim Shalom), or about ending on peace as a posture, not a restatement.
3. **Elohai N'tzor.** Kavanah already names the turn from fixed prayer to private meditation and the move from "we" to "I." My caption echoes it closely. A shorter caption could instead foreground the *silence to add our own words* (the closing rubric), which neither currently owns.
4. **Vayechulu.** Kavanah: "We rise and say it together, standing as witnesses." My caption repeats "rise / together / witnesses" almost verbatim. This one most needs a rewrite or a trim — the rising is also cued by the choreography glyph, so the caption is free to say something else entirely (e.g., the return of sound after the silent Amidah).
5. **Bar'chu** (reference caption). Note the approved caption and the kavanah both hinge on "the moment [a room of] individuals becomes a community." This overlap was approved as-is, so it sets the *ceiling* on acceptable overlap — anything tighter than Bar'chu should be fine; anything looser than the four above should be reconsidered.

### Three least-confident captions

1. **Mi Chamocha.** This is not a standalone accordion unit — it lives *inside* Emet v'Emunah (same content file, same kavanah), and the section opener lists it separately. If the rail treats it as its own unit it needs a caption; if not, drop it. My caption also risks colliding with the kavanah, which already calls it "the song Moses and Miriam sang at the sea." **Needs Daniel's call on whether it's a unit at all.**
2. **V'ahavta & Tzitzit.** Same structural problem — embedded in the Sh'ma file with no separate kavanah. The opener lists it; the content doesn't break it out. My caption assumes it *is* rail-visible. If it is, confidence is low because there's no kavanah to dedupe against, so the caption is doing all the interpretive work alone (and "the answer" framing leans on Ahavat Olam's "we love because we have first been loved," which is a different unit).
3. **The Silent Amidah.** There's no per-unit kavanah here — only a section rubric ("we rise and pray these seven blessings, each at our own pace"). My caption restates the rubric. It reads more as a section-opener line than a per-unit caption, and it will sit adjacent to "We Open Our Lips," which is the real first unit. Daniel may want this slot to carry the *three-steps-in / bow* choreography note instead, or to be blank and let "We Open Our Lips" be the first captioned unit.

### Two notes on scope

- **Unit count.** Final: 13 (Kabbalat) + 10 (Sh'ma, incl. Mi Chamocha and V'ahavta & Tzitzit as confirmed rail sub-marks) + 11 (Amidah, incl. The Silent Amidah rail unit) + 5 (Concluding) + 2 back matter (Seasonal Insertions, Kiddush) = **41 rail captions.** ALT pairs resolved (ALT A kept for both Seasonal Insertions and Kiddush; ALT B deleted). The machine-readable dictionary below carries 41 entries — one per registry rail unit — and excludes "We Open Our Lips," which is not a registry unit.
- **Seasonal Insertions** is one accordion unit in the FULL doc but contains six sub-prayers (Ya'aleh V'yavo, Al HaNisim ×2, Ten Days, Omer, Festival Kiddush). Per Daniel (2026-07-05) it stays ONE rail entry — no per-sub-prayer captions.
