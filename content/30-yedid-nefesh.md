# Yedid Nefesh · יְדִיד נֶפֶשׁ

## kavanah
A 16th-century love song by R. Elazar Azikri of Safed — the soul chasing after Shabbat as the Beloved.

## hebrew
### V1
יְדִיד נֶפֶשׁ, אָב הָרַחֲמָן, מְשֹׁךְ עַבְדְּךָ אֶל רְצוֹנֶךָ.
יָרוּץ עַבְדְּךָ כְּמוֹ אַיָּל, יִשְׁתַּחֲוֶה אֶל מוּל הֲדָרֶךָ.
יֶעֱרַב לוֹ יְדִידוּתֶךָ, מִנֹּפֶת צוּף וְכָל טָעַם.
### V4
הִגָּלֶה נָא וּפְרֹשׂ חָבִיב עָלַי, אֶת סֻכַּת שְׁלוֹמֶךָ.
תָּאִיר אֶרֶץ מִכְּבוֹדֶךָ, נָגִילָה וְנִשְׂמְחָה בָךְ.
מַהֵר אֱהוֹב כִּי בָא מוֹעֵד, וְחָנֵּנוּ כִּימֵי עוֹלָם.

## translit
### V1
Yedid nefesh, Av harachaman, meshoch avdecha el retzonecha.
Yarutz avdecha kemo ayal, yishtachaveh el mul hadarecha.
Ye'erav lo yedidutecha, minofet tzuf v'chol ta'am.
### V4
Higaleh na ufros chaviv alai, et sukkat sh'lomecha.
Ta'ir eretz mik'vodecha, nagilah v'nism'cha vach.
Maher ahuv ki va mo'ed, v'chaneinu kimei olam.

## translation
**V1:** *soul's beloved — compassionate one — pull me toward what you want. I'll run to you like a deer, fall at the light of your face. Your love is sweeter to me than honey, than anything.*

**V4:** *show yourself, please — spread over me, beloved, your canopy of peace. Let the whole earth shine with your presence; we'll dance and delight in you. Hurry, dear one — the time is here — be with us, like in the old days.*

## commentary
- °Elazar Azikri wrote *Yedid Nefesh* in Safed in the 1500s and recorded it in his diary as a private mystical love poem, never meant for the prayerbook. It escaped his notebook and became one of the most beloved openings of Kabbalat Shabbat — a reminder that some of our most public liturgy began as someone's secret. *[spike sample note — for Daniel's review]*
